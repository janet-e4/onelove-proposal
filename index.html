The Problem

A Great Business with a Website That's Working Against It

The current site isn't just outdated — it's actively costing the salon new customers every single day.

One Love Pet Salon is, by any honest measure, a genuinely excellent business. It has the reviews to prove it, the staff tenure to back it up, and the kind of loyal customer base that takes years to build. Customers like Lisa S., who has been coming for 12 years with her dog Bruce. Customers like S.W., who has been coming for 9 years with a fidgety dog who has a limp leg — and keeps coming back because the team is always patient and gentle.

That reputation is real. The problem is that a prospective customer who finds the current website has no way of knowing any of it. The site is a thin GoDaddy builder template with no gallery, no staff story, no reviews surfaced on the page, and no clear path to action. It signals "small and dated" rather than "trusted local expert."

In a market where the competition — PetSmart, Banfield, Z Dog Grooming, Desert Pet Spa — leads with polished mobile design, online booking, and active photo galleries, the website is the single weakest link in an otherwise strong business.

The rebuild doesn't invent a new story. It tells the one that already exists — loudly, clearly, and in a way that converts a first-time visitor into a first-time customer.

The Emerging Threat

AI Search Is Changing Who Gets Found — and Who Doesn't

Beyond traditional Google, there's a growing problem: AI assistants are becoming the first place people go for local recommendations. And the current site is invisible to them too.

More and more Las Vegas pet owners are opening ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity and asking something like "What are the best dog groomers near me in Summerlin?" These AI systems don't just scrape Google results — they pull from structured web content: properly marked-up pages, consistent business information, and fresh content that signals an active, credible business.

A site with no schema markup, no service pages, no location structure, and no fresh content is essentially invisible to AI answer engines. One Love has the reviews and the reputation to rank well in AI recommendations — but only if the website gives those systems something to work with. Right now it doesn't.

PetSmart has a structured website. Banfield has a structured website. The independents investing in their web presence are getting surfaced in AI answers. One Love is not.

The rebuild includes a proper local SEO and AEO foundation: LocalBusiness schema, service schema, consistent NAP across the site, location-specific language, and a content structure that AI systems can read and cite. The optional SEO + AEO optimization add-on takes this further with a dedicated pass specifically targeting AI answer-engine visibility.

This isn't speculative. AI-driven local search is already happening. The question is whether One Love is positioned to benefit from it — or whether a competitor is.
